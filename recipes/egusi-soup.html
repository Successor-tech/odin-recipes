Egusi Soup

Description

Egusi Soup is a popular West African dish made with ground melon seeds, vegetables, and meat or fish. It is rich, flavorful, and traditionally served with pounded yam, fufu, or rice.

This recipe highlights the savory taste of egusi combined with your favorite proteins and leafy greens.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup ground egusi (melon seeds)
  • 1/2 cup palm oil
  • 1 cup chopped spinach or bitter leaf
  • 1/2 cup chopped onions
  • 1-2 cups stock or water
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp pepper
  • Optional: meat, fish, or crayfish

Steps

  1. Heat palm oil in a pot over medium heat.
  2. Add chopped onions and sauté for 2-3 minutes.
  3. Add ground egusi and stir continuously to avoid burning.
  4. Add stock or water gradually, stirring to create a smooth mixture.
  5. Add meat, fish, or crayfish if using, and cook until tender.
  6. Add chopped spinach or bitter leaf and cook for another 5 minutes.
  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  8. Serve hot with pounded yam, fufu, or rice.
